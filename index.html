WHAT IS THIS ?

This is a simulation of celestial bodies with a Keplerian motion. Watch a (small) mega-constellation orbiting Earth, discover the wonders of the Jovian system with its various moons, explore the Solar System as a whole or even other stellar systems beyond imagination!

Code development is underway to provide both a user-friendly UI and an efficient physics engine to compute all the data needed.

SO HOW DO I...

Get started with the current SCENARIO or else, choose or create one (WIP). You might want to have a look on the simulation's SETTINGS before starting (WIP).

You can use + and - keys to control time, Z/S, Q/D and A/E to move the camera around, P to show/hide bodies' SOI

Toggle [ and ] to switch the origin body (with all motion relative to it)

If you don't have any access to a keyboard, you can also choose the focus (center point) using the Body Arborescence, rotate the camera with a swipe on your device screen in any direction, pinch and zoom with two fingers' gesture, toggle SOI with a click on the body.

WHAT ELSE CAN I DO ?

For now that's quite all. The tool will later provide the ability to compute and execute maneuvers, instantaneous or continuous from an orbit to another. It could also provide a n-body simulation of a short trajectory so that the inaccuracy of Keplerian motion can be assessed. Orbital decay simulation using atmospheric drag would also be a big feature to implement.
